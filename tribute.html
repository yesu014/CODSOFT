Dr. MANMOHAN SINGH

1932-2024

FATHER OF INDIAN ECONOMIC REFORMS

Dr. Manmohan Singh, born on 26th September 1932, is a distinguished Indian economist, academician, and politician who served as the 13th Prime Minister of India from 2004 to 2014. Renowned for his economic acumen and integrity, Dr. Singh played a pivotal role in shaping modern India's economic policies. He is often hailed as the architect of India's economic liberalization in the early 1990s, which set the foundation for the country's rapid growth and development.

A recipient of numerous accolades, including the Padma Vibhushan, Dr. Singh's contributions span decades of public service, academia, and governance. Known for his humility, intellect, and visionary leadership, he remains an iconic figure in India's history.

BIOGRAPHICS

Early Life

  • Born: September 26, 1932, in Gah, Punjab (now in Pakistan).
  • Completed his early education in Punjab before Partition.
  • Immigrated to India during Partition in 1947.

Education

  • Bachelor’s degree in Economics from Panjab University, Chandigarh.
  • Master’s degree in Economics from Panjab University, Chandigarh.
  • Attended the University of Cambridge, where he earned his Economics Tripos.
  • Completed his D.Phil. in Economics from the University of Oxford in 1962.

Professional Career

  • Began his career as a lecturer at Panjab University and later at the University of Delhi.
  • Worked for the United Nations Conference on Trade and Development (UNCTAD) during the 1960s.
  • Served as Chief Economic Advisor in the Ministry of Finance in India (1972-1976).
  • Governor of the Reserve Bank of India (1982-1985).

Political Career

  • Appointed as Finance Minister of India (1991-1996).
  • Known for initiating landmark economic reforms in 1991, liberalizing the Indian economy.
  • Became the Prime Minister of India from 2004 to 2014, serving two consecutive terms.
  • During his tenure, India experienced significant economic growth and global integration.

Achievements and Honors

  • Known as the architect of India’s economic reforms in the early 1990s.
  • Received the Padma Vibhushan in 1987 for his contributions to public service.
  • Recognized globally for his leadership and economic expertise.

Personal Life

  • Married to Gursharan Kaur, with whom he has three daughters.
  • Known for his simplicity, humility, and integrity.
